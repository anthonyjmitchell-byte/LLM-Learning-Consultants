Composite Scores Overview

Key Findings

  • High Usage: Students heavily use LLMs (Usage_Student mean ~ 2.1 / 3.0)
  • Moderate Learning Gains: Learning_Student mean ~ 1.7 / 3.25
  • Perceived Benefits: Relatively high (Benefit_Student mean ~ 1.25 / 1.75)
  • Risk Level: Moderately high (Risk_Student mean ~ 2.5 / 3.0)
  • Problematic behavior remains relatively low on average (Behavior_Student mean ~ 0.45 / 1.0)

Distribution of Composite Scores

Conclusions

CS students at Lewis University demonstrate high usage and strong perceived benefits from Large Language Models. However, the actual learning gains appear more moderate in comparison to usage levels.

This pattern suggests that many students are currently using LLMs more for productivity and task completion rather than for deep conceptual understanding and long-term learning.

Recommendations

For Faculty: Develop clear guidelines and best practices for responsible LLM usage in Computer Science courses. Consider integrating LLM literacy into the curriculum to help students convert high usage into stronger learning outcomes while managing potential academic risks.
