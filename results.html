Survey Analysis Results: Students vs Alumni

All composite scores in this section are normalized to a 0–1 scale for fair comparison between current undergraduate students and alumni/graduates.

Figure 9: Student LLM Impact Profile (Normalized 0–1 Scale)

Detailed Interpretation:
The radar chart for current Computer Science students reveals a distinct profile with prominent spikes in Usage and Dependency. These high scores indicate that today’s undergraduates have deeply integrated LLMs into their daily academic routines. In contrast, the Learning Impact spoke is noticeably shorter, reflecting limited perceived gains in deep conceptual understanding, critical thinking, and long-term retention.

Figure 10: Alumni LLM Impact Profile (Normalized 0–1 Scale)

Detailed Interpretation:
The alumni radar chart shows a more balanced but still concerning profile. Alumni report lower Dependency and Usage compared to current students, while the Learning Impact spoke remains short and weak — similar to that of current students.

Figure 11: LLM Impact Comparison – Students vs Alumni

Detailed Interpretation:
Current students score substantially higher on Usage Frequency and LLM Dependency. Both groups show strikingly low scores on Learning Impact. This consistent weakness across cohorts strongly exposes a systemic gap between rapid LLM adoption and meaningful improvements in core Computer Science competencies.
